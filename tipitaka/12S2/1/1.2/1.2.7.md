## Summary

Acelakassapasutta records a conversation between the Buddha and the naked Kassapa, who asks the Buddha about the nature of dukkha. The Buddha rejects Kassapa's questions about dukkha, and instead teaches the middle way between Eternalism and Nihilism using dependent origination. Kassapa is impressed by the Buddha's teaching, take the three refuges and asks to be ordained in the Buddha's presence. As he had formerly belonged to another sect (of naked ascetics), he chose to undergo probation for 4 years rather than the required 4 months. A short time after ordination, Kassapa became arahataá¹ƒ (awakened).

## Commentary

This sutta has a tetralemma that illustrates that "self" and "dukkha" are disjointed concepts that should not be linked together. The middle way that the Buddha teaches is the dependent origination, between Eternalism and Nihilism.

This sutta is interesting in that it may imply the Buddha can still experience dukkha after awakening, but the Buddha is able to "unconstruct" experienced dukkha, and hence extinguish and eliminate it. This is supported by the Buddha clearly experiencing dukkha in his latter years, falling sick, troubled by events, becoming old and eventually dying, so he is clearly subject to jarÄmaraá¹‡aá¹ƒ and hence dukkha. Therefore the elimination of dukkha is a psychological elimation, the removal of the mental results of stimuli and physical processes, not the avoidance of them. This also implies cessation or extinguishment are ongoing activities and not once-off.

It is also interesting that it discloses one of the Buddha's rules regarding ordination - religious practitioners from other sects must undergo four months probation before they can be fully ordained.
